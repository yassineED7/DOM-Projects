Vagabond Insights

What is the essence of Musashi's way of the sword?
The essence is not about killing; it is about understanding life and reaching a state of complete mental clarity, "as empty as the wind."
How did Musashi's view on strength change?
He started out wanting to be "invincible under the sun" by the sword, but he later realized true strength is harmony with nature and people.
What does "growth" mean in the world of Vagabond?
Growth is a painful process of letting go of the ego and freeing oneself from pride and attachment to the past.
What was Takuan's lesson that changed Musashi?
He taught him that the sword should not be a tool for destruction, and that a man can be "complete" rather than just a fighter.
Why does Musashi seek solitude?
Solitude is not isolation; it is a retreat to test the self and face truths that cannot be seen amidst the noise of others.
